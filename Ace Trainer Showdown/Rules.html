Core Rules

  1. Cards must have a Regulation Mark (Sword & Shield onwards).
  2. All Pokémon V and Pokémon ex are banned.
  3. You may only run 2 copies of each card in your deck.
  4. Each evolution line in your deck must be a different type.
  5. (IRL play only) At the start of the game, each player may view their prize cards and exchange a card from their hand.

Exceptions & Exemptions

  1. If a card specifically requires another card of the same type to function properly, you may run 1 copy of each card.
  2. If a card references itself, you may run up to 4 copies of that card provided no other printings are used.

Example 1: Plusle (Fusion Strike) has an attack that requires Minun. You may run 1 Plusle and 1 Minun rather than 2 Plusle in your deck.

Example 2: Finneon (Lost Origin) accelerates Energy to Pokémon with the Swim Freely attack. You may run 1 "Swim Freely" Seadra, Dewgong, and Seaking in addition to 1 Finneon. Kingdra does not have Swim Freely, so it is not allowed.

Example 3: Kirlia (Chilling Reign) has Mirage Step, allowing you to Bench 3 additional Kirlia from your deck. You may run up to 4 Mirage Step Kirlia, but may not run any Refinement Kirlia if more than 1 Mirage Step Kirlia is in the deck.

Even Further Beyond

As a custom format, we've already modified the rules. If there's something you'd rather change at your local table, go for it! While not officially a part of this unofficial format, we present Trainer Stars, a set of additional deckbuilding restrictions to choose from. Due to tighter deckbuilding requirements, cards from the Sun & Moon block are also legal in this mode (and they have Regulation Marks in Japan, actually!).

  • Regional Master

    All Pokémon and Supporters must be from the same region (e.g. "Johto").

  • Highlander

    All Trainer cards are reduced to a 1-copy limit. Pokémon remain at 2, but must be different printings. You may not utilize the self-reference exception.

  • Block Master

    All cards in the deck must come from the same block (Sun & Moon, Sword & Shield, or Scarlet & Violet).

Taking on these restrictions is not without reward. For each Trainer Star added to your deck, you may run 1 additional ACE SPEC. Each Ace Spec must be a unique type (Item, Tool, Stadium, Energy). Additionally, at the start of the game, you may swap an additional card from your hand and prizes.
